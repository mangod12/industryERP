## Devil's Advocate Notes

Here is what this gets right: the app now has strong browser regression coverage for the client-reported refresh behavior, template upload visibility, and no-import preview behavior. The backend suite is broad and the local deployment served the updated JavaScript contract during verification.

Concern: Python security tooling is not locally reproducible.
Severity: Medium
Framework: Deployment and environment blind spot

What I see:
`pip-audit` and `detect-secrets` are not installed and are not listed in `requirements-dev.txt`.

Why it matters:
Security checks can pass in one environment and be skipped locally, which weakens pre-deploy confidence.

What to do:
Add pinned `pip-audit` and `detect-secrets` entries to `requirements-dev.txt`, then run them in CI and local QA.

Concern: Deprecation warnings are high-volume.
Severity: Medium
Framework: Pre-mortem

What I see:
The full test run completed with 1843 warnings across Pydantic, FastAPI lifespan, `datetime.utcnow()`, and SQLAlchemy legacy APIs.

Why it matters:
The next dependency upgrade can turn these into broken runtime behavior or noisy CI failures.

What to do:
Schedule a compatibility hardening pass for Pydantic V2 config/validators, FastAPI lifespan, timezone-aware UTC, and `Session.get()`.

Concern: Browser QA is strong but still mostly smoke-level for imports.
Severity: Medium
Framework: AI happy-path bias

What I see:
Playwright verifies template visibility and scrap preview safety, but not every downloadable template through preview and commit paths.

Why it matters:
Template drift can still reappear for stage updates, assembly lists, and assembly part lists.

What to do:
Create import-spec contract tests that prove every downloadable template can be parsed, previewed, and either safely committed or rejected with a downloadable error report.

Verdict: Ship with watch items. No blocking QA failures remain after formatting. The remaining issues are maintenance and security-tooling parity, not immediate client-facing regressions.
